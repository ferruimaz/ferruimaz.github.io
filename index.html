Bio

I am an incoming PhD student in Pure Mathematics and Mathematical Statistics at the University of Cambridge, supervised by Sergio Bacallado and advised by Richard Nickl. I am a La Caixa Foundation fellow.

My research lies at the intersection of pure mathematics, statistics, and machine learning. I am currently interested in understanding how transformers perform in-context learning — in particular, how they learn kernel representations when trained to match Gaussian Process posteriors, and what this implies for uncertainty quantification.

Currently, I am completing an MPhil in Mathematics at Cambridge, doing research on similar topics. Previously, I was a research assistant at Aalto University under the supervision of Vikas Garg. I received my BSc in Mathematics from the Universidad de Sevilla, where my final dissertation was on topological data analysis.
